04 What is an Exempt Reporting Advisor (ERA)?
An Exempt Reporting Advisor is an investment adviser that is not required to register with the SEC under the Investment Advisers Act of 1940 but is nonetheless required to submit certain reports to the SEC (Form ADV, Parts 1 and 2) and may be subject to state-level oversight. The ERA framework was established under the Dodd-Frank Act and applies to advisers who manage solely private funds with less than $150 million in assets under management in the United States, or who advise solely venture capital funds. ERA status allows an adviser to operate with a reduced regulatory burden while still maintaining baseline reporting obligations to regulators; the fund's Form ADV filings are available on IAPD (the SEC's investment adviser public disclosure database) and will be provided in the data room upon request.

Section 2 — Draft Horse Capital Partners
11 What is the investment strategy in plain English?
Draft Horse Capital Partners is a private secured private credit fund that deploys a $100 million credit facility to one institutional private equity sponsor operating in the aviation sector. The fund lends against a five-layer collateral package — described in detail in Q14 — that includes operating cash flows, real estate and inventory, portfolio company equity, aviation assets registered under UCC-1 and FAA filings, and a corporate guaranty from the sponsor. Investors receive a fixed tiered yield paid quarterly, ranging from 9% to 17% per annum depending on commitment size, for a three-year term. The fund does not diversify across multiple borrowers; it is a single-credit vehicle, and that concentration is a deliberate structural choice disclosed prominently in the offering documents.
Concentration Risk: This fund is structurally concentrated in a diversified borrower base and a single industry sector. Investors who require diversification at the fund level should evaluate this risk carefully before committing capital.
12 Who is the PE sponsor and why is their identity confidential?
The identity of the PE sponsor borrower is held confidential under the terms of the credit facility agreement. The borrower is an institutional private equity firm operating a commercial charter aviation business with a documented fleet of Gulfstream G550 aircraft independently appraised at $30–$45 million per aircraft. Confidentiality is maintained at the borrower's request, which is common in relationship-sourced bilateral credit facilities where the borrower seeks to avoid public disclosure of private financing arrangements. SCALE LLP has conducted legal due diligence on the borrower's entity structure, collateral ownership, and UCC filing status. Qualifying institutional investors may, under appropriate non-disclosure agreements and at the fund's discretion, receive access to additional counterparty information as part of an enhanced due diligence process.
Disclosure: Investors cannot independently verify borrower creditworthiness without access to the borrower's identity and financial statements. This is a structural limitation of this investment. The fund's five-layer collateral framework is designed to reduce reliance on borrower creditworthiness, but counterparty identity opacity is a risk that should be weighed accordingly.
13 What happens to my investment if the PE sponsor defaults?
In the event of a borrower default, the fund's enforcement process proceeds sequentially through the five-layer collateral package. Each layer is independently perfected and independently enforceable, meaning enforcement does not require all layers to be intact simultaneously. In practice, a default event triggers the following: (1) the fund accelerates the outstanding balance; (2) SCALE LLP manages the UCC-1 and FAA Registry enforcement process against the aviation assets; (3) the equity pledge allows the fund to foreclose on and take ownership of the portfolio companies; (4) real estate and inventory liens are enforced through standard secured creditor remedies; and (5) the PE sponsor guaranty creates direct recourse against the guarantor entity. The fund's stress-case recovery analysis, which is available in the data room, projects materially superior recovery rates compared to single-collateral private credit facilities at equivalent leverage. That said, no collateral structure eliminates default risk, and ultimate recovery is subject to market conditions, asset values, and legal proceedings at the time of enforcement.
14 Walk me through the five layers of collateral.
The five-layer collateral architecture for Draft Horse Capital Partners was structured so that each layer is independently enforceable and structurally uncorrelated to the other layers in stress scenarios:
  • 01Operating Cash Flows: The fund holds a first-priority perfected lien against the operating revenues and receivables of the borrower's business. This provides continuous debt service coverage and is the primary source of scheduled interest and principal payments.
  • 02Physical Real Estate & Inventory: Perfected first liens against real property and tangible business inventory owned by the borrower and its portfolio companies, providing asset-level recovery independent of business performance.
  • 03100% Portfolio Company Equity Pledge: The fund holds a pledged security interest in 100% of the equity interests of the borrower's portfolio companies. Upon default, the fund may exercise its rights to foreclose on and take direct ownership of those entities without requiring judicial proceedings beyond the equity enforcement process.
  • 04Gulfstream G550 Aviation Assets — UCC-1 & FAA Registry First Lien: Hard asset collateral consisting of the borrower's Gulfstream G550 fleet, each aircraft independently appraised at $30–$45 million. Liens are perfected under both UCC Article 9 (for domestic purposes) and the FAA Civil Aviation Registry (for aircraft-specific perfection). This layer represents the most liquid and globally marketable collateral position.
  • 05PE Sponsor Corporate Guaranty: A direct corporate guaranty from the PE sponsor entity, which has equity exposure on both sides of the transaction. This creates an additional recourse layer against the sponsor's balance sheet in the event asset-level recovery is insufficient to satisfy the outstanding obligation.
15 What is the UCC-1 and how does it protect me?
A UCC-1 Financing Statement is a legal document filed under Article 9 of the Uniform Commercial Code that publicly establishes a secured creditor's priority lien against a debtor's personal property collateral. Filing a UCC-1 "perfects" the security interest, meaning the creditor has a legally enforceable, publicly recorded claim that takes priority over subsequent creditors and, in most cases, survives the debtor's bankruptcy. For Draft Horse Capital Partners, SCALE LLP has filed and perfected UCC-1 financing statements against the applicable collateral on behalf of the fund, including the aviation assets, equipment, receivables, and inventory. In a default scenario, the perfected UCC-1 filing gives the fund the legal right to seize, sell, or otherwise dispose of the collateral to satisfy the outstanding debt obligation without relying on the borrower's cooperation, subject to applicable state law enforcement procedures.
16 What is the FAA Civil Aviation Registry filing and why does it matter?
The FAA Civil Aviation Registry maintains the official national record of aircraft ownership and liens in the United States. Under the Federal Aviation Act and the Cape Town Convention (for international transactions), filing a security interest in a civil aircraft with the FAA Registry perfects that interest with respect to third parties globally — including in foreign jurisdictions that are signatories to the Cape Town Convention. This is critically important for aviation collateral because Gulfstream G550 aircraft are mobile assets that can be relocated internationally; FAA Registry perfection ensures the fund's lien follows the aircraft regardless of its physical location. The dual perfection under both UCC-1 and FAA Registry provides the most robust domestic and international priority position available for aircraft collateral, and is the accepted standard for sophisticated aviation finance transactions.
17 How is the yield determined and is it guaranteed?
The yield paid to investors in Draft Horse Capital Partners is a tiered fixed rate ranging from 9% per annum at the minimum commitment level to 17% per annum at the highest commitment tier. These rates reflect the spread the fund earns on the credit facility it extends to the PE sponsor, after accounting for fund operating costs. The yield is fixed at subscription and does not fluctuate with market interest rates during the investment term. However, the yield is not contractually guaranteed in the way a bond coupon might be — it is dependent on the borrower's performance and the fund's ability to collect scheduled payments. If the borrower fails to make scheduled payments and collateral enforcement is required, distributions to investors may be delayed or reduced during the recovery period. The offering documents will specify the precise payment waterfall and the conditions under which distributions may be suspended.
Important: The yield figures disclosed are targets based on the fund's projected operating performance, not guaranteed contractual obligations enforceable regardless of fund performance. Investors may receive less than the stated yield, including possible loss of principal, in a default scenario.
18 Why is the term 3 years with no early redemption?
The three-year term with no early redemption provision matches the maturity of the underlying credit facility extended to the PE sponsor. Private credit facilities of this structure are bilateral, relationship-sourced transactions that require capital certainty for the duration of the facility; the borrower's operational and financial planning depends on the committed capital remaining in place through the agreed term. Early redemption mechanisms would expose the fund to potential liquidity mismatches between investor redemption demands and the illiquid nature of the underlying credit asset. Investors should treat this commitment as fully illiquid for the three-year term and only commit capital they do not require access to during that period.
Liquidity Risk: There is no secondary market for interests in Draft Horse Capital Partners and no early redemption mechanism. Investors must be prepared to hold their investment for the full three-year term.
19 What does "no management fee, no carried interest" actually mean?
Draft Horse Capital Partners charges no annual management fee (typically 1–2% of AUM in conventional funds) and takes no carried interest or performance allocation on investment profits. The fund's economic model is instead structured so that investors receive their full tiered yield and the fund retains the spread between the rate charged to the borrower and the rate paid to investors; the Principal's economic incentive is therefore aligned with deal performance rather than AUM growth. This structure eliminates the fee drag that reduces net investor returns in most private credit and hedge fund vehicles. Prospective investors should nonetheless verify in the offering documents that there are no alternative fees — such as origination fees, administrative fees, or subscription fees — that could reduce net returns, and should request a complete fee schedule from SCALE LLP as part of their diligence.

21 What is the single-counterparty risk and how is it mitigated?
Because Draft Horse deploys its entire $100 million capital base to a diversified borrower base, 100% of investor capital is exposed to the credit performance of one counterparty. This is the fund's primary and most significant structural risk. It cannot be diversified away at the fund level. The mitigation framework is entirely collateral-based: five independently perfected collateral layers provide multiple, structurally uncorrelated recovery mechanisms. Additionally, the covenant framework includes minimum debt service coverage ratios, liquidity reserve requirements, collateral maintenance covenants with regular appraisal triggers, and cross-default provisions — all designed to give the fund early warning and enforcement rights before a default reaches a capital-impairment level. Investors who require counterparty diversification at the fund level should consider Quarter Horse Capital Partners, which deploys across nine distinct asset classes and multiple counterparties.

Section 3 — Quarter Horse Capital Partners
23 How does investing across 9 asset classes reduce risk?
Quarter Horse Capital Partners is structured around nine distinct asset classes, each driven by an independent economic vector, with the goal of creating a cash flow floor that holds across varied market environments. The logic is correlation-based: real estate cycle pressure does not impair charter aviation revenue; commodity price corrections do not impair structured private credit receivables; energy sector disruption does not affect fine art collateral values. When one asset class experiences stress, the other eight continue generating income, reducing the probability that a single economic event impairs the entire fund. Each asset class is additionally backed by hard-asset collateral specific to its category — first-lien real estate, allocated bullion, aircraft under UCC-1, mineral resources, JP Morgan LTV-validated art, proven energy reserves, and government-backed technology credit — providing nine independent recovery mechanisms at the portfolio level.

25 What is the algorithmic trading allocation and what are its risks?
One of the nine asset classes in Quarter Horse Capital Partners includes an allocation to systematic algorithmic trading strategies, which may operate across futures, equities, commodities, or currency markets. Algorithmic trading allocations are distinct from the fund's private credit and hard asset allocations in that they carry mark-to-market risk, are exposed to model failure and technology failure, and can produce drawdowns even in periods when other fund strategies are performing. The offering documents contain specific risk disclosures for this allocation, including maximum drawdown parameters and risk management protocols. Investors should be aware that the algorithmic component introduces a category of risk that differs materially from the collateral-backed private credit strategies that dominate the rest of the fund, and should ask specifically about position sizing, strategy independence, and the stop-loss or risk-off protocols governing this allocation.
Risk Disclosure: Algorithmic trading strategies can experience rapid and significant losses. Model-based strategies may perform differently in live markets than in historical backtests. This allocation is subject to risks that do not apply to secured private credit investments.

31 What happens if one asset class underperforms? Do I still get my yield?
Quarter Horse Capital Partners is a portfolio-level vehicle, meaning investor distributions are funded from the aggregate cash flows generated across all nine asset classes rather than from any single class in isolation. If one asset class underperforms or fails to generate its projected return, the shortfall must be covered by excess performance from other classes or absorbed through a reduction in the aggregate distribution rate. The fund does not guarantee investors a fixed yield regardless of portfolio performance; the yield figures disclosed are targets based on the projected combined performance of the full asset class portfolio. The offering documents specify the distribution waterfall, the conditions under which distributions may be reduced, and the reserve mechanisms (if any) that buffer short-term performance variation.
Yield Risk: Actual distributions to investors depend on the realized performance of the underlying asset classes and are not guaranteed at the disclosed target rate. Investors may receive distributions below the target yield if portfolio performance is below projections.
