Section 1 — Platform Overview
Q1
What is Dark Horse Wealth Management?
Dark Horse Wealth Management LLC is a Texas-based private family office and investment management firm that serves as the governing entity for the Dark Horse fund family. It operates three fund vehicles — Corinthian Capital Fund LLC, Draft Horse Capital Partners LLC, and Quarter Horse Capital Partners LLC — each managed through dedicated subsidiary management companies. The firm was founded by Caleb Caraway and maintains its principal offices in Granbury, Texas.
Q2
What funds does the platform operate?
The platform operates three private debt funds. Corinthian Capital Fund LLC is the operational proof-of-concept vehicle offering a 2% monthly fixed return. Draft Horse Capital Partners LLC is the flagship multi-strategy fund targeting a $1 billion raise across five investment verticals. Quarter Horse Capital Partners LLC is a deal-by-deal fund targeting $120 million, 100% owned by the Managing Principal.
Q3
Who manages the funds?
Caleb Caraway serves as Managing Principal and Chief Investment Officer across all three funds. Each fund is managed through its own dedicated management company: Corinthian Capital Partners LLC manages the Corinthian fund, Draft Horse Wealth Management LLC manages Draft Horse, and Quarter Horse Wealth Management LLC manages Quarter Horse.
Q4
What is the investment thesis?
The platform deploys capital into uncorrelated, appreciating asset classes and cash-flowing businesses — engineered for high compression ratios, consistent fixed-rate returns, and investment opportunities backed by tangible, appreciating collateral. Each investment is selected for three characteristics: the underlying asset must be appreciating, the investment must generate contractual cash flow, and every position must be backed by tangible collateral.
Q5
What asset classes does the platform invest in?
The platform invests across five verticals: aviation (via Pegasus Capital Partners LLC), mining, metals, and energy (via Stateside Resources LLC), e-commerce private credit and private equity (cash-flowing businesses), real estate (via Dark Horse RE Holdings LLC), and fine art (via the DHWM art collection and Art Bond SPV).
Q6
Is Dark Horse Wealth Management a registered investment adviser?
Dark Horse Advisers LLC, a sibling entity to DHWM under the same parent structure, is in the process of becoming a fully registered investment adviser (RIA) that will manage oversight of investments for the entire Dark Horse portfolio. All three fund vehicles have filed Form D with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission under Regulation D.
Q7
What are the service providers?
Legal Counsel: SCALE LLP (Dori Karjian). Auditor and Tax Advisor: Spicer and Jefferies. Fund Administrator: NAV Fund Services. Banking: Axos Bank.
Q8
How does the fee structure work across all funds?
All three funds charge zero management fees, zero performance fees, and zero carried interest. This structure eliminates fee drag entirely and ensures that GP and investor interests are aligned through principal capital commitment rather than fee extraction.
Section 2 — Corinthian Capital Fund LLC
Q9
What is Corinthian Capital Fund LLC?

Q13

Q14

Corinthian has maintained consistent fixed-rate distributions to investors throughout its operational history, validating the private debt strategy that is now being deployed at greater scale through Draft Horse Capital Partners.
Q15
How does Corinthian relate to Draft Horse?
Corinthian is the operational predecessor to Draft Horse. It demonstrated the lending thesis at a smaller scale, establishing a track record before the launch of the larger multi-strategy vehicle. The two funds operate independently with separate management companies.

Section 5 — Fees and Economics
Q33
What are the management fees?
Zero across all three funds. None of the Dark Horse fund vehicles charge annual management fees.
Q34
What are the performance fees?
Zero across all three funds. There is no carried interest, performance allocation, or incentive fee of any kind.
Q35
How does the GP earn money?
The GP earns through the spread between what borrowers pay the fund and what the fund pays investors. The GP's economic incentive is aligned with deal performance and borrower repayment rather than assets under management.
Q36
Are there any other fees?
Prospective investors should verify in the offering documents that there are no origination fees, administrative fees, or subscription fees that could reduce net returns. The zero-fee structure applies to management and performance fees specifically.
Q37
How does the zero-fee structure benefit investors?
A traditional 2-and-20 structure on a fund generating a given gross return delivers materially less to investors after management fees and carry. With zero fees, every basis point of return flows directly to investors. This eliminates the single largest drag on investor returns in the alternative investment industry.
Q38
Does the GP invest alongside investors?
Yes. The Managing Principal commits personal capital alongside investors, ensuring alignment between GP and investor outcomes.
Section 6 — Regulatory and Compliance
Q39
What is Reg D 506(c)?
Rule 506(c) under Regulation D permits general solicitation and advertising of the offering, provided all investors are verified accredited investors. Draft Horse Capital Partners operates under this exemption.
Q40
What is Reg D 506(b)?
Rule 506(b) under Regulation D prohibits general solicitation but allows up to 35 sophisticated non-accredited investors. All investors must have a pre-existing substantive relationship with the issuer. Corinthian Capital Fund and Quarter Horse Capital Partners operate under this exemption.
Q41
What is the Section 3(c)(1) exemption?
All three funds rely on the Section 3(c)(1) exemption from registration under the Investment Company Act of 1940, which limits each fund to no more than 100 beneficial owners.
Q42
Have Form D filings been made?
All three fund vehicles have filed Form D with the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission under Regulation D.
Q43
Is there regulatory oversight?
As exempt funds managed by management entities that file as Exempt Reporting Advisors with the applicable state securities authority in Texas, the funds do not receive the same level of regulatory oversight that applies to SEC-registered investment advisers and registered investment companies. Dark Horse Advisers LLC is in the process of becoming a fully registered RIA.
Q44
What reporting do investors receive?
Investors receive quarterly reports covering fund performance, asset-level updates, distribution summaries, and material developments. Audited annual financial statements are provided following each fiscal year-end. The auditor is Spicer and Jefferies.
Section 7 — Risk Factors
Q45
What are the principal risks?
Investing in private debt funds involves substantial risk including possible loss of the entire investment. Key risks include borrower default, asset valuation fluctuation, illiquidity, concentration by vertical or borrower, regulatory changes, and the absence of a secondary market for fund interests.
Q46
Are the returns guaranteed?
No. All stated returns are contractual targets and are not guaranteed. Distributions are contingent on borrower performance and fund cash flow. The funds may not achieve target returns in any given period.
Q47
Can I redeem early?
There is no public market for interests in any of the three funds and no general early redemption mechanism. Investors should expect to hold their investment for the applicable fund term. Quarter Horse investors hold per-deal until each deal matures or exits.
Q48
What happens if a borrower defaults?
The fund would pursue recovery through the applicable contractual provisions and legal remedies available under the lending agreements. Recovery timelines and amounts are not guaranteed and depend on borrower circumstances and applicable law.
Q49
How is risk managed across verticals?
Draft Horse deploys across five uncorrelated verticals — aviation, mining, real estate, e-commerce, and fine art — which provides diversification at the portfolio level. Each vertical is accessed through a dedicated portfolio company with domain-specific expertise, and each operates with isolated liability structures.
Q50
Are past performance results indicative of future returns?
No. Past performance of Corinthian Capital Fund or any related strategy does not guarantee future results for any fund vehicle. Each fund should be evaluated independently based on its offering documents, structure, and risk factors.
