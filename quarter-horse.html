Fund Overview

Fixed returns, deal by deal.

Texas LLC · Reg D 506(b) · Section 3(c)(1)
Quarter Horse Capital Partners LLC is a deal-by-deal private debt fund offering investors fixed-rate returns across individual lending opportunities. Each deal has its own terms, duration, and rate. The fund is 100% GP-owned with a zero-fee structure. This is a private lending fund — investors receive a fixed interest rate only. Capital is deployed into tangible-asset lending opportunities sourced through a proprietary pipeline spanning aviation, mining and metals, art-backed securities, real estate, and custom institutional allocations. Pre-existing substantive relationship required for all investors.

Quarter Horse Capital Partners LLC — 506(b) Disclosure
Quarter Horse Capital Partners LLC is a Texas limited liability company. Securities are offered pursuant to Regulation D, Rule 506(b) under the Securities Act of 1933. The fund relies on the exemption from registration under Section 3(c)(1) of the Investment Company Act of 1940 and is limited to no more than 100 beneficial owners. A pre-existing substantive relationship with the fund or its management is required for all investors. Up to 35 sophisticated non-accredited investors may participate. No general solicitation or general advertising is permitted. All materials are indicative only and subject to the definitive Private Placement Memorandum. Stated targets are not guaranteed returns. Principal is subject to loss.
Risk Disclosures
All investments involve risk, including the possible loss of principal. Private debt investments are illiquid and may not be suitable for all investors. There is no public market for the securities offered. Each deal carries its own terms, duration, and return profile. Past deal performance is not indicative of future results. Investors should be prepared to bear the economic risk of their investment for the full term of each deal. The fixed-rate return on each deal is subject to the creditworthiness of the underlying borrower and the value of any collateral. There is no guarantee that any deal will perform as projected. Concentration risk, credit risk, liquidity risk, and market risk are inherent in all fund investments.
