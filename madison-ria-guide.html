1 Why a New LLC — Not Dark Horse Wealth Management Caleb + Dori

Dark Horse Wealth Management (DHWM) is structured as a private family office — the governing entity at the top of Caleb's organizational hierarchy. Registering it directly as an RIA creates three significant problems:

Problem 1 — Family Office Exemption Risk

  • The SEC's "family office" exemption under the Investment Advisers Act excludes entities that only advise family members from RIA registration requirements
  • If DHWM begins serving external clients, it may lose that exemption and create unexpected federal registration obligations
  • Keeping DHWM as a pure family office preserves flexibility and avoids regulatory entanglement

Problem 2 — Regulatory Examination Exposure

  • SEC-registered investment advisers are subject to periodic examination by the SEC's Office of Compliance Inspections and Examinations (OCIE)
  • Putting that examination risk inside DHWM — the top-of-house entity that controls everything — exposes the entire structure to regulatory scrutiny
  • A dedicated advisory entity limits examination scope to that entity only

Problem 3 — Structural Clarity and Scalability

  • As Caleb's fund platform grows, the advisory entity needs to clearly delineate its role relative to each active fund
  • Multiple funds with different investor bases are cleaner when managed by a purpose-built advisory entity
  • SEC registration from day one is cleaner with a dedicated RIA entity that has a defined, auditable advisory scope
Bottom line: Do NOT register DHWM as the RIA. A dedicated entity — Dark Horse Advisers LLC — is the correct structure.

4 Registration Level: SEC Registration — Direct Path (Skip State) Caleb + Dori

Dark Horse Advisers LLC will register directly with the SEC — bypassing SEC registration entirely. This is the correct and legally appropriate path given Caleb's stated AUM trajectory.

Why SEC (Not SEC)

  • Caleb's AUM target: $100M+ by end of 2026 (personal target: $120M). At $100M–$110M RAUM, SEC registration is no longer available — SEC registration becomes mandatory.
  • Adil Kassam's committed capital: Partner Adil Kassam has committed $1B+ in capital. This alone puts the platform well above the SEC threshold from inception.
  • Under the Investment Advisers Act: An adviser that reasonably expects to exceed $100M in RAUM within 120 days of registration may register directly with the SEC. Given Caleb's stated trajectory, this standard is clearly met.
  • No interim SEC registration required: There is no legal obligation to register with SEC first when the SEC path is available and appropriate.

The SEC Registration Threshold

  • Below $100M RAUM: Would normally register with state (e.g., TSSB in Texas) — not applicable here given trajectory
  • $100M–$110M RAUM: Buffer zone — may register with state or SEC
  • Above $110M RAUM: Must register with SEC — mandatory
  • Reasonably expects $100M within 120 days: May register with SEC directly — this is Caleb's path
✓ Decision confirmed: Register Dark Horse Advisers LLC directly with the SEC. Given Adil Kassam's $1B+ capital commitment and Caleb's $100M+ AUM target before end of 2026, SEC registration would be transitional at best and unnecessary at worst. Go straight to SEC.
5 Confirm Madison's Series 65 Status Madison

The Series 65 (NASAA Uniform Investment Adviser Law Examination) is Madison's qualifying license. There is a critical time-based rule Madison must verify:

The 2-Year Clock Rule

  • If Madison passed the Series 65 more than 2 years ago and has not been affiliated with a registered investment adviser since, the exam result may be considered expired
  • If expired, she would need to re-take the Series 65 before her IAR registration can be processed
  • Verify current Series 65 status by logging into finpro.finra.org (FINRA's FinPro Gateway)

If Re-Exam Is Required

  • Series 65 exam fee: ~$187
  • 3-hour exam, 130 questions, 71% passing score required
  • Study time typically 60–120 hours; Kaplan or NASAA prep materials available
  • Schedule through Prometric testing centers
⚠️ Check this FIRST. If the Series 65 is expired, it adds 2–4 months to the timeline. Verify status at finpro.finra.org before any other steps begin.

Unlike SEC registration (which requires a separate SEC Form 133.18 balance sheet), the SEC does not require a separate certified balance sheet submission for Form ADV registration.   • Ensure the firm's bank account is funded and operational before filing
