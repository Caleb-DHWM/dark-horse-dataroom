Phase 0 — Entity Decision: Where Does the RIA Live?

The single most important structural decision before filing anything. This directly affects liability, regulatory exposure, and scalability.

Decide First
A Why NOT to register Dark Horse Wealth Management as the RIA Caleb + Dori Karjian

Dark Horse Wealth Management (DHWM) is structured as a private family office — the governing entity at the top of Caleb's organizational hierarchy. Registering it directly as an RIA creates three significant problems:

Problem 1 — Family Office Exemption Risk

  • The SEC's "family office" exemption under the Investment Advisers Act excludes entities that only advise family members and certain key employees from RIA registration requirements
  • If DHWM begins providing advisory services to external clients (fund LPs, outside investors), it may lose this exemption and create unexpected registration obligations at the federal level
  • Keeping DHWM as a pure family office preserves flexibility and avoids regulatory entanglement

Problem 2 — Regulatory Examination Exposure

  • Registered investment advisers are subject to periodic examination by the TSSB (and SEC when AUM threshold is crossed)
  • Putting that examination risk inside DHWM — the top-of-house entity that controls everything — exposes the entire structure to regulatory scrutiny
  • A dedicated advisory entity limits examination scope to that entity only

Problem 3 — Structural Clarity and Scalability

  • As Caleb's fund platform grows, the advisory entity will need to clearly delineate its role relative to each active fund
  • Multiple funds with different investor bases and strategies are cleaner when managed by a purpose-built advisory entity
  • State-to-SEC transition (when AUM hits $110M) is more straightforward with a dedicated RIA entity
Bottom line: Do NOT register DHWM as the RIA. The structural and regulatory costs outweigh any simplicity gains.
B The Recommended Structure: Dark Horse Advisers LLC Caleb + Dori Karjian

Form a new Texas LLC — recommended name: Dark Horse Advisers LLC (or Corinthian Capital Advisers LLC if branding to the flagship fund). This entity registers as the RIA with the TSSB.

Structure

  • Caleb Caraway: 100% owner / Sole Member — listed as control person on Form ADV Schedule A
  • Madison Cornwell: Manager (advisory) / IAR / Chief Compliance Officer — the registered representative. Madison does not need to be an owner; her role is defined operationally in the Operating Agreement
  • No outside investors: This entity is a management/advisory company, not a fund. It earns management fees or advisory fees; it does not hold investor capital

3 Confirm Madison's Series 65 Status and Eligibility Madison

The Series 65 (NASAA Uniform Investment Adviser Law Examination) is Madison's qualifying license. However, there is a critical time-based rule:

The 2-Year Clock Rule

  • If Madison passed the Series 65 more than 2 years ago and has not been affiliated with a registered investment adviser during that time, the exam result may be considered expired
  • If this applies, she would need to re-take the Series 65 before her IAR registration can be processed
  • Madison should verify her current Series 65 status at FINRA BrokerCheck or by logging into FINRA's FinPro Gateway

If Re-Exam Is Needed

  • Series 65 exam fee: ~$187
  • 3-hour exam, 130 questions, 71% passing score required
  • Study time typically 60–120 hours; Kaplan or NASAA prep materials available
  • Schedule through Prometric testing centers
⚠️ Check this FIRST. If the Series 65 is expired, it adds 2–4 months to the timeline. Do not start the entity formation process until Madison's exam status is confirmed.

Key Items for Dark Horse Advisers LLC

6 Draft the Investment Advisory Agreement(s) Madison + Dori Karjian

The advisory contract is executed between Dark Horse Advisers LLC and each fund (or fund manager entity). Since Caleb advises private funds rather than individual retail clients, these may be structured as fund-level advisory agreements rather than individual client agreements.

Required Contract Provisions

  • Whether the adviser has discretionary authority over the fund's investments
  • Term and termination provisions (typically 30–60 days written notice)

Do NOT reference registration status as a marketing endorsement. Per Texas Securities Act §4004.002, the fact of TSSB registration cannot be used to imply competence or quality of service in any advertisement.
7 Create Compliance Policies and Procedures Manual Madison (CCO)

Written compliance policies are required for every registered investment adviser. Madison, as CCO, owns this document and is responsible for enforcing it.

Required Policies

  • Written Compliance Policies & Procedures: Reasonably designed to prevent violations of Texas Securities Act and Advisers Act rules
  • Code of Ethics: Standards of conduct, personal securities transaction reporting, insider trading prevention, violation reporting
  • Privacy Policy (Reg S-P): How non-public personal information of fund investors is protected and used
  • MNPI Policy: Prevent misuse of material non-public information (especially relevant given Caleb's deal flow activity across multiple sectors)

  • Cybersecurity Policy: Data protection, encryption, incident response

Comment letters are extremely common — especially on first-time filings. Treat them as routine, not as a red flag. Most are resolved in one round of corrections.

Most Common TSSB Comment Letter Issues

  • Inconsistency between Part 1 Item 5.D (client types) and Part 2A Item 7 (types of clients)

  • Discretionary authority in Part 1 Item 8.C inconsistent with Part 2A Item 16
  • Services in Part 1 Item 5.G don't match Part 2A Item 4
  • Missing or incomplete financial disclosures (Items 18.A / 18.B)

24 Prepare for TSSB Examination (expect within Year 1–2) Madison (CCO) + Caleb

The TSSB typically examines newly registered firms within the first 1–2 years. Being "regulatory ready" at all times is the only correct posture.

What Examiners Will Review

  • Form ADV accuracy — are AUM and business description current?
  • Client/fund files — agreements, brochure delivery records, correspondence
  • Compliance manual — is it current, signed, and has the annual review been documented?
  • Code of Ethics — personal securities transaction reports
  • Advertising and marketing materials — any use of registration as an implied endorsement?
  • Fee calculations — are actual fees charged consistent with the disclosed schedule?
  • Books and records completeness and retention
The first examination is the most heavily scrutinized. Do not let books and records fall behind from day one. Madison's CCO obligations are not ceremonial — they are substantive.

25 What Changes at SEC Registration — Madison's Obligations Scale Up Madison + Dori Karjian

Transitioning from state to SEC registration is not a small step. Madison's CCO role becomes materially more complex:

SEC-Specific Additional Requirements

  • Form ADV Part 1 — SEC Filing: Annual updating amendment due within 90 days of fiscal year end; more granular AUM and client disclosures required
  • Advisers Act Compliance: Full suite of SEC Advisers Act rules apply — custody rule (Rule 206(4)-2), performance advertising rule (Rule 206(4)-1 as amended), code of ethics rule (Rule 204A-1)
  • Custody Rule: If the adviser has custody of fund assets (common in private fund context), annual surprise examination by an independent auditor may be required — or the fund must meet the pooled vehicle audit exception
  • Marketing Rule (Rule 206(4)-1): Strict rules on performance advertising, testimonials, and hypothetical performance in marketing materials
  • Form PF: If Caleb's funds qualify as private funds with $150M+ in AUM, Form PF reporting to SEC may be triggered quarterly or annually
  • Examinations: SEC Office of Compliance Inspections and Examinations (OCIE) examinations are more comprehensive than TSSB examinations
⚡ Plan for this now. The Corinthian Form D/A is already ~4 months overdue (CIK 0002036278). Before the RIA is live, Dori Karjian should get Corinthian's EDGAR filings current to ensure there are no outstanding compliance issues that would surface in an examination.
