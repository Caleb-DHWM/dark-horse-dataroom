VI. Single-Counterparty Concentration Risk Disclosure
Most Material Risk — Read Completely: The Fund's entire $1B deployment is concentrated in a diversified borrower base relationship. This is the most material risk in Draft Horse Capital Partners and must be evaluated fully by every prospective investor before committing capital. Credit risk is not diversified across counterparties. Any material adverse change in the borrower's financial condition, operating performance, management continuity, legal status, or industry environment could impair the Fund's ability to recover principal and accrued interest regardless of collateral architecture. In severe stress, correlation between collateral layer values held against a single enterprise is likely higher than diversified portfolio analysis suggests.
Bilateral Monitoring Advantage — The Structural Corollary to Concentration Risk: The single-borrower structure provides the Fund with monitoring capabilities structurally unavailable in diversified portfolios or syndicated facilities. Draft Horse is the sole creditor in a direct, bilateral relationship — with real-time covenant visibility, no syndicate agent intermediary, no information delay, and full contractual authority to act immediately on any deterioration signal. In a broadly syndicated credit facility with 30+ lenders, material adverse changes are frequently identified only after formal default. In Draft Horse's bilateral structure, the Fund detects and responds at the earliest possible stage. Concentration risk and monitoring advantage are two sides of the same structural decision. Both are disclosed. Both must be weighed.
VII. Sovereign Investor Tax Considerations — IRC §892
ItemDetail
IRC §892 ExemptionSovereign wealth funds and government-affiliated institutional investors may be entitled to exemption from U.S. federal income tax on certain types of income under Section 892 of the Internal Revenue Code. Eligibility depends on the specific nature of the Fund's income, the investor's sovereign status, and applicable law at the time of distribution.
UBTI Carve-OutSection 892 exemptions do not extend to Unrelated Business Taxable Income (UBTI) or income from certain commercial activities. Sovereign investors should confirm with their own tax counsel whether Fund income constitutes qualifying §892 income.
FATCA DocumentationNon-U.S. investors must provide applicable FATCA documentation (IRS Form W-8BEN, W-8BEN-E, or W-8EXP as appropriate). Sovereign entities qualifying under IRC §892 typically file Form W-8EXP. Spicer Jeffries LLP coordinates with prospective investors on FATCA and §892 documentation requirements.
No Fund Tax RepresentationThe Fund makes no representation regarding the U.S. federal or state tax treatment of distributions to sovereign or foreign investors. Sovereign and institutional investors must obtain independent tax counsel on the U.S. tax treatment of their investment before committing capital.
General Tax TreatmentFund distributions may be treated as ordinary income subject to applicable federal and state income tax rates. Pass-through entity treatment applies. Annual K-1 prepared by Spicer Jeffries LLP. Investors are solely responsible for their own tax obligations.
