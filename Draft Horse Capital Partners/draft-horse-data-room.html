A Different Kind of Fund
To Prospective Investors,

The alternative investment industry has settled into a familiar pattern: charge two-and-twenty for access to a narrow band of correlated strategies, and let fee income compound regardless of whether investors see meaningful returns. Draft Horse Capital Partners was built on the conviction that this model is broken — and that a better one is possible.

Draft Horse Capital Partners LLC is a multi-strategy private debt fund structured under Rule 506(c) of Regulation D. The fund deploys capital across five distinct tangible-asset verticals — aviation, mining and metals, e-commerce private credit, real estate, and fine art — through dedicated operating entities with domain-specific expertise. Investors receive a fixed contractual rate of return, paid on a regular distribution schedule. The structure is straightforward: you lend, the fund deploys, and you receive your contractual return.

We charge no management fees and no performance fees. This is not a promotional feature or a temporary concession. It is a structural commitment embedded in the fund documents. The rationale is simple: fee elimination removes the single largest drag on investor returns in the alternative space. A traditional two-and-twenty structure on a fund generating strong gross returns delivers a fraction of that to investors after fees are extracted. At Draft Horse, gross returns flow directly to investors.

The fund targets a one-billion-dollar raise deployed across five verticals, each accessed through a purpose-built operating entity. Aviation capital flows through Pegasus Capital Partners LLC. Mining and metals investments are managed through Stateside Resources LLC. Real estate positions are held through Dark Horse RE Holdings LLC. Art-backed securities are issued through a dedicated Art Bond SPV. E-commerce private credit is originated directly by the platform. Each entity isolates liability and enables strategy-specific governance, while the platform provides unified oversight.

The thesis is grounded in tangible assets. Every dollar deployed by the fund is backed by something real — aircraft, mineral rights, authenticated fine art, real property, or verified commercial cash flows. These are asset classes that institutional portfolios chronically underweight, yet they provide the inflation protection, downside support, and income generation that sophisticated allocators increasingly seek.

Draft Horse Capital Partners LLC is managed by Draft Horse Wealth Management LLC, the institutional investment platform of the Caraway family office. I hold seventy-five percent of the management company alongside Adil Kassam, who holds twenty-five percent and leads our sovereign wealth and institutional capital relationships. The family-office structure provides the speed and conviction of a principal investor with the compliance infrastructure and reporting standards that institutional allocators require.

We believe the most compelling opportunity in private markets today is not another fund charging fees for access to the same crowded trades. It is a disciplined, zero-fee vehicle that puts institutional capital to work in the tangible-asset strategies that deliver genuine diversification and contractual income. That is what Draft Horse was built to provide.

We welcome the opportunity to discuss the fund in detail and look forward to your engagement with the materials in this data room.

Fund Overview
Multi-Strategy Private Debt
Draft Horse Capital Partners LLC is a multi-strategy private debt fund structured under Rule 506(c) of Regulation D. The fund provides qualified investors with diversified exposure to five tangible-asset verticals through a single institutional-grade vehicle with zero management fees and zero performance fees.
Investment Thesis
Traditional alternative allocations cluster around the same asset classes — private equity, venture capital, and commercial real estate — producing increasingly compressed returns with high fee drag. Draft Horse targets tangible-asset verticals that institutional portfolios chronically underweight: aviation, natural resources, private credit, real estate, and fine art. By building dedicated operating entities in each vertical and eliminating all fees, the fund delivers diversified alternative exposure with full alignment between manager and investor.
Fund Structure
Investors in Draft Horse Capital Partners are lenders to the fund. The fund deploys capital across its five verticals and pays investors a fixed contractual rate of return on a regular distribution schedule — monthly or quarterly depending on subscription terms. Investors do not participate in equity upside or share profits from underlying investments. The return is contractual and predetermined at the time of subscription. This structure provides income certainty and separates investor economics from the variable performance of individual portfolio holdings.

Vertical I
Aviation
Operating Entity: Pegasus Capital Partners LLC
$320M

Strategy

Pegasus Capital Partners LLC acquires, leases, and manages commercial and private aircraft. The aviation vertical generates revenue through two primary channels: contracted lease income from aircraft placed with operators, and long-term asset appreciation driven by persistent global demand-supply imbalance in the aviation fleet market.

Global aviation demand continues to outpace fleet supply, creating favorable economics for aircraft owners and operators. Aircraft are tangible, globally mobile assets with established secondary markets and transparent valuation frameworks.

Deployment Approach

Capital is deployed into the acquisition of aircraft assets selected based on maintenance status, remaining useful life, operator creditworthiness, and lease term economics. Pegasus targets aircraft types with strong secondary market liquidity and predictable residual value trajectories.

The entity manages the full lifecycle of each asset: acquisition, operator placement, lease management, maintenance oversight, and eventual disposition. This vertically integrated approach maximizes income generation while maintaining asset quality.

Strategy

The private credit vertical provides direct lending to established e-commerce businesses with verifiable cash flows. E-commerce operators generate predictable revenue streams documented through digital transaction data — platform sales records, payment processor statements, and logistics data — enabling precise, data-driven underwriting.

This vertical generates current income through structured credit facilities with fixed rates. The digital nature of e-commerce cash flows provides real-time transparency into borrower performance, enabling proactive portfolio management and early identification of credit deterioration.

Underwriting Approach

Each credit facility is underwritten on verified digital cash flows, with structural protections including collateral coverage and cash flow covenants. The platform evaluates borrower operating history, revenue consistency, margin stability, customer concentration, and platform diversification before extending capital.

The e-commerce lending market offers attractive risk-adjusted returns because traditional banks are structurally disadvantaged in underwriting digital-native businesses. The data transparency inherent in e-commerce operations gives specialized lenders an informational advantage that supports disciplined credit selection.

Strategy

The fine art vertical encompasses two complementary activities: direct art acquisition for the Dark Horse portfolio, and the issuance of art-backed securities through a dedicated Art Bond SPV. Fine art represents a significant global market with historically low correlation to traditional financial assets, providing genuine portfolio diversification.

Art Bonds are fixed-income instruments collateralized by authenticated, independently appraised fine art collections. The structure combines the predictability of fixed-income coupon payments with the inflation-hedging characteristics and intrinsic value of physical art assets.

Art Bond Structure

The Art Bond SPV issues fixed-income securities backed by the appraised value of art collections held in custody. Each issuance undergoes independent authentication and appraisal, with collateral coverage ratios designed to provide bondholder protection. Coupon payments are sourced from fund-level cash flows, with the underlying art collection serving as collateral support.

The platform targets building a significant art portfolio through disciplined acquisition focused on works with established provenance, stable valuation histories, and long-term appreciation potential. Art assets held by the fund further diversify the tangible-asset base supporting investor returns.

Risk Factors
Material considerations.
Investing in Draft Horse Capital Partners involves significant risks. The following risk factors are material to an investment decision and should be carefully considered. This is a summary; the Private Placement Memorandum contains a comprehensive discussion of all applicable risks.
Illiquidity Risk
Fund interests are illiquid and not freely transferable. There is no public market for the interests, and none is expected to develop. Investors should be prepared to hold their investment for the full fund term. Redemption rights, if any, are limited and subject to the terms of the PPM.
No Guarantee of Returns
While investors receive a contractual fixed rate of return, the fund's ability to make distributions depends on the performance of underlying investments. There is no guarantee that the fund will generate sufficient returns to meet its obligations. Investors may lose some or all of their investment.
Vertical Concentration
The fund deploys capital across five verticals, but within each vertical, positions may be concentrated in a limited number of investments. Adverse performance of a single holding within a vertical could have a material impact on that vertical's contribution to fund returns.
Tangible Asset Valuation
The fund's verticals are anchored by tangible assets including aircraft, mineral rights, fine art, and real property. The value of these assets is subject to market fluctuation, appraisal uncertainty, and changes in supply-demand dynamics that may not be immediately reflected in reported valuations.
Commodity Price Risk
The mining, metals, and energy vertical is directly exposed to commodity price volatility. Silver, energy, and other commodity prices are influenced by global macroeconomic conditions, monetary policy, geopolitical events, and supply disruptions that are outside the fund's control.
Aviation Market Risk
The aviation vertical is subject to risks specific to the global aviation industry, including demand cyclicality, regulatory changes, maintenance cost escalation, operator credit risk, and residual value uncertainty. Geopolitical events and public health crises can cause rapid demand deterioration.
Private Credit Risk
E-commerce lending involves borrower default risk, cash flow deterioration, and platform dependency. Changes in e-commerce marketplace policies, consumer spending patterns, or competitive dynamics could impair borrower ability to service debt obligations.
Real Estate Market Risk
Real estate investments are subject to market cycles, interest rate sensitivity, tenant risk, regulatory changes, and local market conditions. Property values and rental income can decline due to economic downturns, oversupply, or changes in demand fundamentals.
Art Market Risk
Fine art valuations are subjective, illiquid, and influenced by taste, cultural trends, and collector demand. The art market lacks the transparency and standardization of financial markets. Authentication disputes, provenance challenges, and changes in market sentiment can materially impact values.
Regulatory Risk
The fund operates under Regulation D exemptions which impose restrictions on investor eligibility and transfer of interests. Changes in securities law, tax regulation, or industry-specific regulation could impact fund operations, investor eligibility, or the economics of underlying investments.
Key Person Risk
The fund depends significantly on Caleb Caraway as Managing Principal and Chief Investment Officer. The departure or incapacity of key personnel could adversely affect investment decision-making, portfolio management, and investor relations.
Limited Operating History
Draft Horse Capital Partners is a newly formed fund. The fund's investment strategy, while informed by the platform's existing experience, has limited track record specific to this vehicle. Past performance of related entities is not indicative of future results for this fund.
