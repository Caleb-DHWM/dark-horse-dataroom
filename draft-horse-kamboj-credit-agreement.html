Recitals

A. The Borrower is an active private equity operating company engaged in aviation charter operations, fleet management, real estate investment, and portfolio company management, with an established track record of generating material monthly returns across its portfolio.

B. The Lender is a private credit fund that deploys institutional capital into private lending opportunities with asset-rich operating companies. The Lender has committed to provide the Borrower with a private credit facility in the aggregate principal amount of Ten Million United States Dollars (USD $10,000,000.00) (the "Credit Facility").

C. The Borrower desires to borrow the Credit Facility from the Lender under the terms and conditions set forth herein, and the Lender desires to extend such Credit Facility to the Borrower subject to the security package, representations, covenants, and conditions described below.

D. The Parties acknowledge that the Lender is providing institutional private credit — not equity investment — and accordingly, the monthly return rate of 8% per month is structured and justified on the following basis:

  • The Lender does not share in the tax advantages available to private equity investors in the Borrower's portfolio companies, including but not limited to Qualified Small Business Stock (QSBS) exclusions under IRC §1202, carried interest treatment, and portfolio company depreciation pass-throughs;
  • The Lender does not participate in the back-end exit waterfall when portfolio companies are sold, which is known to be substantial and represents a significant component of total equity investor returns;
  • Equity investors in the Borrower's portfolio companies are understood to earn materially higher effective monthly returns than the rate provided herein, by virtue of their participation in both operating distributions and exit proceeds — none of which accrue to the Lender as a creditor;
  • The Lender is committing a material capital deployment that entitles the Lender to private equity equivalent economics whilst retaining the security, priority, and legal protections of a private creditor position;
  • The Credit Facility rate of 8% per month represents fair compensation for the Lender's foregone equity upside and tax advantages, while affording the Borrower the structural and balance sheet benefits of debt financing over equity dilution.

8. Lender Capital Recovery Protocol

The Parties acknowledge that the Lender's investors require certainty of capital recovery in the event of a default. This Section establishes the mandatory, time-bound process by which the Lender shall recover all outstanding Obligations from the Collateral following an Event of Default, and the Borrower's binding cooperation obligations in furtherance thereof.

(a) Liquidation Waterfall — Order of Collateral Enforcement. Upon the occurrence of an Event of Default and expiration of any applicable cure period, the Lender shall enforce against the Collateral in the following priority sequence, proceeding to the next layer only if the prior layer is insufficient to satisfy all outstanding Obligations in full:

  1. Layer 1 — Operating Cash Flows and Receivables (Days 1–30): The Lender shall immediately direct all operating cash flows, receivables, and bank account balances subject to Account Control Agreements to be applied to the outstanding Obligations. The Borrower shall cause all account debtors to redirect payments to the Lender's designated account within 5 business days of written notice. Target recovery timeline: 30 days from acceleration notice.
  2. Layer 2 — Real Estate and Inventory (Days 1–60): Simultaneously with Layer 1 enforcement, the Lender may direct the immediate listing and sale of all real property Collateral at fair market value through a licensed broker selected by the Lender. The Borrower shall execute all transfer documents, deeds, and closing instruments within 5 business days of the Lender's written request. Target recovery timeline: 60 days from acceleration notice.
  3. Layer 3 — Portfolio Company Equity Interests (Days 1–60): The Lender may immediately exercise its rights under the portfolio company pledge agreements, including directing distributions, forcing a sale of the pledged interests to a third party, or taking control of voting rights to facilitate an orderly sale. The Borrower shall deliver all original pledge certificates, operating agreements, and transfer instruments within 5 business days of written demand. Target recovery timeline: 60 days from acceleration notice.
  4. Layer 4 — Aviation Fleet — Current (Days 1–45): The Lender may immediately engage an aviation asset broker of its choosing to list and sell the Gulfstream G4-class aircraft subject to the Lender's lien. The Borrower shall provide full cooperation, including delivery of all aircraft logbooks, maintenance records, and title documents, and shall make each aircraft physically available for inspection within 48 hours of written request. Target recovery timeline: 45 days from acceleration notice for the two unencumbered aircraft.
  5. Layer 5 — Future Aviation Fleet (Subordinated): The Lender's subordinated second-priority lien on any future G550-class aircraft shall be enforced following satisfaction of Quarter Horse Capital Partners' first-priority rights, with proceeds applied to any remaining unsatisfied Obligations.Credit insurance proceeds shall be applied to any remaining balance after enforcement of Layers 1–5.

(b) Proceeds Application Order. All proceeds recovered through enforcement of the Security Package shall be applied in the following order: (i) costs and expenses of enforcement, including reasonable attorneys' fees, broker commissions, and court costs; (ii) accrued and unpaid default interest; (iii) accrued and unpaid Monthly Returns; (iv) outstanding principal balance; (v) any surplus after full satisfaction of all Obligations shall be promptly returned to the Borrower.

(c) Mandatory Borrower Cooperation Timeline. Upon receipt of a written acceleration notice from the Lender following an Event of Default, the Borrower shall, without further demand:

  1. Within 48 hours: Make all aircraft physically available for inspection; provide all aircraft records and title documents to the Lender;
  2. Within 5 business days: Execute all Account Control Agreement activation notices; deliver all portfolio company pledge certificates and transfer instruments; provide a complete and current asset schedule with valuations;
  3. Within 10 business days: Execute all real property transfer documents and deeds as directed by the Lender; provide all keys, access codes, and property management contacts;
  4. Within 15 business days: Deliver a complete accounting of all cash, receivables, and liquid assets available for immediate application to the Obligations.

(d) Appointment of Receiver. In the event the Borrower fails to cooperate with the capital recovery process in accordance with the timelines set forth in Section 8(c), or in the event the Lender has reasonable grounds to believe that Collateral is being dissipated, transferred, or concealed, the Lender shall be entitled to seek the immediate appointment of a court-appointed receiver over all Collateral on an emergency ex parte basis, without prior notice to the Borrower and without the requirement to post bond. The Borrower hereby irrevocably consents to such appointment and waives any right to contest the appointment of a receiver in such circumstances.

(e) Lender Control of Liquidation. The Lender, acting in its sole reasonable discretion, shall control all aspects of the liquidation process, including the selection of brokers, appraisers, auctioneers, and legal counsel. The Borrower shall have no right to direct, delay, or interfere with the liquidation process following an Event of Default, except to provide the cooperation required under Section 8(c).

(f) Investor Disclosure. The Borrower acknowledges that the Lender may disclose the existence and general terms of this Capital Recovery Protocol to its investors in anonymized form as part of its investor due diligence materials, without identifying the Borrower by name.

(g) No Marshaling. The Lender shall not be required to marshal any Collateral or to resort to any particular Collateral in any particular order.
