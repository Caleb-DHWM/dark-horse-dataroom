Quarter Horse Capital Partners is a Texas-governed private credit fund deploying institutional capital across seven uncorrelated SPV lending verticals — spanning real estate, precious metals trade finance, mining, aviation, energy & technology infrastructure, fine art, and structured private credit. The fund pays a tiered, stated annual return to investors on a quarterly basis with zero management fees, zero performance fees, and zero carried interest. All excess earnings above the stated investor return are retained by the fund, creating a transparent, aligned structure purpose-built for sovereign and institutional capital.

Why Quarter Horse
We Are the Bank. The SPVs Are the Operators.
The Fund deploys as private lender to seven controlled SPVs and operating companies. Each SPV manages its own industry vertical and returns principal + distributions to the Fund. Clean SPV architecture. Liability isolation. Institutional credit model.
Absolute Zero Fee Structure
No management fee. No performance fee. No carried interest. Every basis point of investor return goes directly to investors — a structure uncommon at any AUM level in private credit. The Fund's 19.14% blended gross yield maintains meaningful spread above the 17% investor tier.Seven independent SPV collateral pools.
Fine Art SPV — Four Revenue Streams
The $150M Fine Art SPV generates income four ways: (1) natural appreciation 6–16%/yr; (2) JP Morgan art-private lending at 600–900 bps spread; (3) museum lease income with zero carrying cost (museum covers insurance + storage); (4) sale proceeds including the December tax-demand cycle — when family offices globally deploy fiscal year-end capital into professionally appraised art at Art Basel Miami Beach.
