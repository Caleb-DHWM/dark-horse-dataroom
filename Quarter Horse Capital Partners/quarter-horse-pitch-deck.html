The Investment Case
The Sovereign Capital Case
Why diversified hard-asset private credit — now
📉
Public Markets Volatility
Equity market correlation has increased across traditional asset classes. Sovereign investors are actively seeking uncorrelated return streams that deliver consistent yields irrespective of public market cycles.
🏦
Bank Credit Contraction
Regional bank retraction from middle-market lending — real estate bridge loans, energy, and specialty credit — has created a structural supply gap in private credit that disciplined direct lenders can fill at premium yields.
⚡
Energy Infrastructure Demand
AI-driven data center buildout, grid modernization, and nuclear renaissance are generating extraordinary debt capital demand for energy technology companies — with attractive risk-adjusted returns for first movers.
🔶
Hard Asset Premium
Inflation persistence, dollar reserve dynamics, and geopolitical uncertainty are sustaining a premium on hard-asset collateral — real estate, gold, aviation, mining, and fine art — across institutional portfolios globally.
Quarter Horse's answer: A single fund accessing all of these tailwinds simultaneously — 7 SPV lending verticals, one stated return, zero fee drag, hard-asset collateral across every allocation, managed by a principal with an established private credit track record.

Why Both? The Institutional Allocation Case
Quarter Horse and Draft Horse are designed as complementary allocations within an institutional portfolio. Quarter Horse provides broad, diversified exposure across 7 SPV lending verticals with a transparent tiered return — ideal for sovereign capital seeking uncorrelated multi-asset income. Draft Horse provides concentrated, deep-conviction exposure for institutions seeking higher absolute returns through selective, high-diligence positioning. Together, they represent the full spectrum of Caleb Caraway's private credit platform under Dark Horse Wealth Management.
