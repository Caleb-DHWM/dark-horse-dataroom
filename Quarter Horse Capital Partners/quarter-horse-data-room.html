Investment Strategy

Deal-by-Deal Approach

The core investment philosophy of Quarter Horse is transparency and investor optionality. Unlike traditional blind pool funds, Quarter Horse operates on a deal-by-deal basis where every opportunity is presented individually to qualified investors, who then choose which deals to participate in. This structure provides several key advantages:

No Blind Pool: Each deal is fully underwritten and documented before being presented. Investors review the complete term sheet, borrower profile, collateral description, and risk factors for each opportunity.

Investor Choice: Qualified investors are not required to participate in every deal. This allows investors to align their participation with their risk tolerance, liquidity needs, and diversification goals.

No Unfunded Commitments: Investors are not required to commit capital that sits uninvested. Capital is deployed only when an investor chooses to participate in a specific deal at closing.

Fixed Deal Economics: Each deal has its own fixed contractual rate, duration, distribution schedule, collateral description, and risk profile.

Comprehensive Documentation: A detailed deal memo is prepared for each opportunity, providing all material terms and risk factors necessary for an informed investment decision.

Full Transparency: All terms are disclosed in advance of commitment. Investors have sufficient time and information to evaluate each opportunity before deciding to participate.

Operating Infrastructure: Dark Horse maintains operational teams within each vertical to monitor borrower performance, manage collateral, and enforce covenants. This provides ongoing asset monitoring and reduces reliance on third-party service providers for portfolio management.

Fund Economics: The fund's operating model is supported by the spread between the interest rate charged to borrowers and the rate paid to investors. Borrowers pay a rate that reflects the underwriting effort, ongoing portfolio monitoring, collateral management, and default servicing costs. Investors receive a substantial portion of the borrower's interest payment with minimal deductions for fund operations.

Why Zero Fees: The zero-fee structure reflects the Managing Member's confidence in deal quality and personal capital deployed in every opportunity. Caleb Caraway has no incentive to grow assets under management for fee purposes. Instead, his returns are aligned directly with investor returns through his personal capital participation. Larger deals do not generate higher fees—they simply provide more capital and proportional returns to all participants.

Industry Comparison: Traditional private debt funds typically charge 1-2% annual management fees plus 15-20% carried interest. This structure incentivizes assets gathering and may create pressure to deploy capital regardless of opportunity quality. Quarter Horse's zero-fee approach eliminates these misaligned incentives and ensures the Managing Member's primary objective is deal quality and investor returns.

Regulation D Rule 506(b) Framework

Quarter Horse Capital Partners is offered exclusively to qualified investors under Regulation D, Rule 506(b) of the Securities Act of 1933. This regulatory framework provides important protections and structures for both the fund and its investors.

What is Rule 506(b): Rule 506(b) is an exemption from the SEC registration requirements for securities offerings. It allows issuers to offer and sell securities without registering with the SEC, provided that the offering meets specific conditions regarding investor qualifications and communications.

Pre-Existing Substantive Relationship: Under Rule 506(b), the fund may not conduct general solicitation or general advertising. Instead, all investor inquiries must come from persons with a pre-existing substantive relationship with the Managing Member or the fund. A pre-existing substantive relationship typically means prior business or investment experience with the Managing Member or demonstrated knowledge of and investment experience in similar private offerings.

No General Solicitation: Quarter Horse opportunities are presented exclusively through direct communication to qualified investors with established relationships. No public advertising, media solicitation, or general marketing of individual deals is permitted.

Investor Composition Limits: Rule 506(b) permits unlimited accredited investors to participate. Additionally, up to 35 non-accredited investors may participate, provided they are "sophisticated investors" who have sufficient knowledge and experience in financial and business matters to evaluate the merits and risks of the investment.

Verification Requirements: The fund must verify accreditation status through appropriate documentation, including tax returns, investment account statements, professional certifications, or other reliable evidence. Non-accredited sophisticated investors must be evaluated based on their financial circumstances and investment experience.

Why 506(b): The Managing Member selected the 506(b) framework specifically to emphasize the importance of direct personal relationships with investors. This structure ensures that all Quarter Horse investors have substantive familiarity with the Managing Member and the fund's investment process. It also allows for direct communication and personalized deal presentation without the constraints of a larger public offering.

Investor Eligibility & Qualification

Participation in Quarter Horse deals is limited to qualified investors who meet specific criteria under Regulation D Rule 506(b) and who maintain a pre-existing substantive relationship with the Managing Member or the fund.

Accredited Investor Definition: An accredited investor under SEC Rule 501 is a natural person who has either: (a) individual income exceeding $200,000 or joint spousal income exceeding $300,000 in each of the prior two years with reasonable expectation of the same in the current year; or (b) a net worth exceeding $1,000,000 (excluding the value of the investor's primary residence). Alternatively, certain institutional investors, such as banks, insurance companies, investment companies, employee benefit plans, charitable organizations, and business entities, are automatically accredited.

Sophisticated Investor Definition: A sophisticated investor is a non-accredited investor who has sufficient knowledge and experience in financial and business matters to evaluate the merits and risks of an investment in the fund. There is no bright-line definition of sophistication; instead, it is evaluated on a case-by-case basis considering the investor's financial and investment background, employment history, and experience with similar private offerings.

Pre-Existing Relationship Requirement: All investors must have a pre-existing substantive relationship with the Managing Member or the fund. New investors are typically introduced by existing investors or have prior business relationship or investment experience with Caleb Caraway or Dark Horse Wealth Management. Investors must demonstrate their pre-existing relationship at the time of investment.

Subscription Process: Interested qualified investors submit a completed investor questionnaire confirming accredited or sophisticated status and attesting to a pre-existing substantive relationship. The Managing Member reviews qualifications and approves investors who meet fund criteria. Upon approval, investors execute the subscription agreement and fund documentation, then deploy capital to participate in selected deals.

Verification: The fund maintains records of investor qualifications and accreditation verification documentation. All investors are required to update accreditation information annually and notify the fund immediately of any changes in status that would affect eligibility.

Risk & Compliance

Risk Factors

Quarter Horse investments involve substantial risk of loss. Prospective investors must carefully evaluate the following risk factors before committing capital. An investment in Quarter Horse is suitable only for accredited and sophisticated investors who can afford to lose their entire investment and do not require access to the funds for any foreseeable period.

Deal-Specific Risk
Each deal has unique risks related to the borrower, industry, collateral, and market conditions. Returns depend entirely on borrower performance and the value of deal-specific collateral. Investors should carefully evaluate the underwriting and terms of each individual deal before committing capital.
Illiquidity Risk
Investments in Quarter Horse deals are highly illiquid. There is no secondary market for investor interests, and investors cannot withdraw or transfer their interests without the Managing Member's consent. Investors should not anticipate liquidity until deal maturity or early prepayment.
Concentration Risk
Investors may concentrate capital in a single deal or in a small number of deals. This concentrates both opportunity and risk. While the deal-by-deal structure allows diversification, each individual investor's portfolio may be concentrated based on their selection of deals.
Credit / Default Risk
Borrowers may default on their obligations. Default can result in loss of interest income, principal recovery delays, and potential principal loss. The fund's ability to recover defaulted principal depends on collateral value and remediation effectiveness.
Collateral Valuation Risk
Deal repayment depends partially on collateral value. Collateral may decline in value due to market conditions, deterioration, technological obsolescence, or other factors. In a default scenario, collateral value may be substantially less than at origination.
Key Person Risk
Caleb Caraway, as sole Managing Member, is essential to the fund's operations, investment decisions, and portfolio management. Loss of or incapacity of the Managing Member could adversely affect deal performance and investor returns.
Platform Risk
Quarter Horse's deal pipeline depends on the performance and stability of the Dark Horse platform. Adverse developments affecting Dark Horse's operations, reputation, or financial condition could impact deal origination and pipeline quality.
Regulatory Risk
Changes in securities laws, commodities regulations, or other regulatory frameworks could restrict the types of deals Quarter Horse can originate, require additional disclosures, or result in regulatory enforcement actions that affect fund operations.
Interest Rate / Market Risk
Market interest rate changes do not affect fixed-rate deals already funded, but rising rates could impact borrower creditworthiness and refinancing capacity. Market dislocations could affect collateral values and deal economics.
Tax Risk
Tax treatment of distributions and potential basis adjustments could affect the after-tax returns of the investment. Tax law changes could adversely affect tax efficiency. Investors should consult their tax advisors regarding individual tax consequences.

Conflicts of Interest

The Managing Member maintains multiple business interests and roles that create potential conflicts with Quarter Horse and its investors. These conflicts are disclosed below and are managed through clear disclosure, deal-specific evaluation, and fiduciary obligations to investors.

Platform Ownership: Caleb Caraway owns and controls the Dark Horse Wealth Management platform from which Quarter Horse sources all of its deals. Dark Horse operations receive compensation through the spread between borrower interest rates and investor rates. This creates an incentive to originate deals even when returns may be marginal. The Managing Member addresses this conflict by rigorous deal evaluation and personal capital in every opportunity.

Portfolio Company Involvement: Some deals may involve Dark Horse portfolio companies or entities in which Dark Horse maintains equity interests or operational roles. This creates potential conflicts between Dark Horse's interests as a principal and Quarter Horse investors' interests as creditors. Such deals are fully disclosed in deal-specific materials, and all material conflicts are presented to investors.

Multiple Fund Participation: The Managing Member may sponsor or manage other private debt or investment vehicles that participate in the same deals as Quarter Horse. Capital from multiple vehicles may be combined in a single deal. This is disclosed in deal documentation, and allocation methodology is transparent.

Related Party Transactions: Some deals may involve borrowers, collateral owners, or other parties with relationships to the Managing Member or Dark Horse. These related party relationships are fully disclosed, and the Managing Member commits to applying the same underwriting standards and investor protections as would apply to independent transactions.

Disclosure & Management: The Managing Member discloses all material conflicts in the deal-specific offering materials. Investors are provided with complete information about conflicts and the Managing Member's financial interests in each deal. The Managing Member's fiduciary duty to Quarter Horse investors requires that all conflicts be disclosed and that the Managing Member act in investors' interests in spite of any conflicts.

Risk & Compliance

Regulatory Compliance & AML/KYC

Quarter Horse Capital Partners operates in compliance with all applicable federal and state securities laws, anti-money laundering regulations, and other legal requirements. Compliance is a core organizational responsibility.

Anti-Money Laundering (AML) & Know Your Customer (KYC): All investors are subject to comprehensive KYC procedures including identity verification, beneficial ownership certification, and source of funds verification. Investors must provide documentation confirming their identity and the legitimate source of capital being invested. Ongoing monitoring is conducted to detect suspicious activity or changes in investor status.

OFAC Screening: All investors and borrowers are screened against OFAC (Office of Foreign Assets Control) sanctions lists to ensure compliance with international sanctions programs. Screening is conducted at account opening and periodically throughout the relationship.

Regulation D Filing Requirements: Form D is filed with the SEC within 15 days of the first sale of fund interests, and annual amendments are filed as required. Form D provides notice of the offering and confirms compliance with Rule 506(b) requirements.

State Blue Sky Compliance: The fund complies with applicable state securities laws and blue sky requirements. Where required, state-specific filings and exemptions are obtained. Investors' home states are verified, and all state-specific requirements are met.

Ongoing Reporting Obligations: The Managing Member maintains records of all investor qualifications, accreditation verification, and subscription documentation. Annual updates to accreditation information are requested from all investors. The fund maintains compliance records and is prepared for regulatory inquiries or examination.

Investment Company Act Exemption: The fund relies on the Section 3(c)(1) exemption from the Investment Company Act, which requires that no more than 100 beneficial owners of fund interests exist. The fund monitors the number of investors and complies with this limitation.
